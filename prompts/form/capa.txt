MODULE CONTEXT
A CAPA (Corrective and Preventive Action) is a formal action plan created to address the root cause of a problem and prevent recurrence. Corrective actions fix what went wrong. Preventive actions stop potential problems from occurring. CAPAs are a primary closure mechanism in EHS — they turn identified issues into structured, trackable fixes.

FIELD REFERENCE
Title: A concise name for the corrective or preventive action.
Description: What the action entails and what problem it addresses.
Type: Whether this is a corrective action (fixing a known problem) or a preventive action (stopping a potential problem from occurring).
Priority: The urgency assigned to completing the action.
Severity: The seriousness of the underlying issue being addressed.
Due Date: The deadline by which the action must be completed.
Category: The classification of the issue being addressed.
Department: The organisational unit responsible for carrying out the action.
Status: The current stage of the action — open, in progress, completed, etc.
Assigned To: The individual accountable for executing the action.
Linked Incident ID: A reference to the incident or event that triggered this CAPA, if applicable.
