MODULE CONTEXT
The risk module is used to proactively identify, assess, and manage potential hazards before they cause harm. A risk record represents something that could go wrong — not something that has already happened. It works through a structured assessment to determine how likely and how serious the risk is.

FIELD REFERENCE
Risk Title: A concise name for the risk — e.g., "Chemical Spill Risk", "Fall from Height at Warehouse Mezzanine".
Risk Description: What could happen, why, and the potential impact if the risk materialises.
Risk Context: Operational background, contributing factors, or relevant history that makes this risk relevant now.
Likelihood: The probability that the hazard will result in harm. Values range from Rare through Unlikely, Possible, and Likely to Almost Certain.
Impact / Consequence: The potential severity of harm if the risk materialises. Values range from Insignificant through Minor, Moderate, and Major to Catastrophic.
